What technology is?

The importance and significance of technology in both our personal and professional life is expanding all the time. Understanding how people shape technology and how technology shapes people's interactions with one another and with the natural world is important not only for those who research, develop, and implement new technologies, but also for all the people and organizations who must use those technologies in their professional and personal lives.


The term "technology" is not a neutral term. Depending on their point of view and the circumstances, various people have interpreted it differently. Technology is concerned with determining how knowledge might be creatively applied to organized tasks involving humans. Below, I have explained all different types of technology.


Different Types of Technology


  1. Information Technology

    2. Everything that organizations do with computers is referred to as information technology (IT). Building communications networks for a company, safeguarding data and information, creating and administering databases, assisting employees with computer or mobile device problems, or performing a variety of other tasks to ensure the efficiency and security of business information systems are all examples of information technology. Demand for experts in this industry is high and expanding, and those joining the field have a variety of career routes to select from.


    Fig: Information Technology
  2. Nuclear Technology

    3. Nuclear technology is the study of nuclear processes in atomic nuclei. Nuclear reactors, nuclear medicine, and nuclear weapons are examples of noteworthy nuclear technology. It is also used in smoke detectors and gun sights. Nuclear energy is the energy contained within an atom's nucleus, or core. The compact nucleus of an atom has a tremendous amount of energy. In reality, the force that holds the nucleus together is referred to as the "strong force." Nuclear energy may be utilized to generate electricity, but first it must be liberated from the atom. Atoms are divided to release energy during the nuclear fission process.


    Fig: Nuclear Technology
  3. Bio-Technology

    4. Biotechnology is a branch of technology that uses biological systems, live creatures, or components of them to develop or produce various goods. Brewing and baking bread are examples of biotechnology processes (the use of yeast ( live organism) to generate the desired output). Traditional biotechnology methods often use living creatures in their native state (or further improved via breeding), but more current biotechnology will generally include a more advanced alteration of the biological system or organism. Biotechnology now encompasses a wide range of fields (eg. genetics, biochemistry, molecular biology, etc.). Every year, new technologies and products are produced in fields such as medical (the creation of novel medications and therapies), agriculture (the development of genetically modified plants, biofuels, and biological therapy), and industrial biotechnology (production of chemicals, paper, textiles and food).


    Fig: Bio-Technology
  4. Electronic Technology

    5. The use of scientific ideas and concepts in the design, manufacture, installation, testing, servicing, usage, and control of electrical and electronic parts, equipment, and systems is known as electronics technology. Electronics technology is employed in virtually every industry, including commercial, residential, and industrial. Electronic technology includes devices and equipment such as cell phones, computers, and tablets, circuit making and so on.


    Fig: Electronic Technology
  5. Communication Technology

    6. Communication technology is the use of technology to send messages or information between people or machines. Information processing may assist humans in making decisions, solving problems, and controlling machines. The understanding, skills, and tools that served as the foundation for previous and present communication technology also serve as the foundation for new and improved technologies. All of the inputs, processes, outputs, and feedback related with sending and receiving messages or information are included in a communication system. The message to be send is input. The method by which the message is transported is referred to as the process. The message receipt at the other end is output. Feedback might be about static or clarity.


    Fig: Communication Technology
  6. Material Technology

    7. Materials technology is a broad topic that ranges from the manufacture of items from raw materials through the processing of materials into the shapes and forms required for specific purposes. Materials, such as metals, polymers, and ceramics, often have quite varied characteristics, which implies that the processes used to produce them are fundamentally different. Materials technology is a subject that is continually changing, and new materials with unique features lead to new applications. For example, combining different materials into composites results in completely new material characteristics.


    Fig: Material Technology
  7. Medical Technology

    8. Medical technology is described as the use of science to the development of solutions to health problems or challenges such as illness prevention or delay, or the promotion and monitoring of good health. Medical technology is a critical strategic aspect in the hospital's positioning and perception in the competitive environment of healthcare providers. A plethora of exciting new biomedical gadgets and systems are constantly being released. They are being implemented at a time when hospitals are under increasing pressure to save costs. Forecasting the deployment of medical technology and the ability to continually assess its impact on the hospital, as a result, need the hospital's willingness to make the commitment and give the support for such a program.


    Fig: Medical Technology
  8. Mechanical Technology

Mechanical engineering technology is the application of engineering concepts and technical advances to the manufacture of usable machinery or products. Parts and assemblies are often analyzed using software tools such as Finite Element Analysis and/or Computational Fluid Dynamics. Using Computer Aided Design tools, 3D models may be created to depict components and assemblies. The models may also be utilized directly by software of computer-aided manufacturing to provide "instructions" for the fabrication of things represented by the models, using computer numerically controlled machining or other automated processes.


Fig: Mechanical Technology
